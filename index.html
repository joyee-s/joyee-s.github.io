About Me

Hello! I'm Jayeeta, a passionate Life Science postgraduate based in Kolkata, West Bengal. My M.Sc. in Immunology sparked a deep interest in protein and cell biology, especially within the world of immuno-oncology.

This passion was at the heart of my Master's project, where I dived deep into the tumour microenvironment of breast carcinoma. I focused on T-cell exhaustion, analyzing markers like PD-1, Tim-3, and CD39 in both Swiss Albino mouse models and human clinical samples.

This research gave me a fascinating first-hand look at how T-cells get "tired" from chronic stimulation in tumors, which helps cancer evade our immune system. It really nurtured my interest in understanding T-cell dysfunction and, more importantly, figuring out how we might reverse it to fight cancer more effectively.

This project wasn't just theoretical; it was my technical foundation. I became proficient in multi-parameter flow cytometry, IHC, IF, ELISA, PCR, and Western blotting. More importantly, it honed my skills in experimental design, data analysis, and scientific communication.

I am eager to apply this research experience to an Antibody Development role, contributing to reagent innovation and specificity.
